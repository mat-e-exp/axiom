Technical Architecture

How Axiom Works

AI-powered agent orchestration with deterministic validation gates, full audit logging, and human-in-the-loop approval at every regulatory submission boundary.

Architectural Principle

AI orchestrates. Deterministic code validates.

Axiom operates in regulated financial services, where every submission carries legal and regulatory liability. No regulatory return is generated, validated, or filed on the basis of probabilistic AI output alone. The architecture enforces a clear separation between intelligence and assurance.

AI handles orchestration, not certification

AI agents coordinate data collection, prepare draft returns, and flag anomalies for human review. They accelerate the process and eliminate manual overhead. But they do not certify the output — that responsibility sits with deterministic validation gates and authorised signatories.

Hard validation gates are code, not inference

XBRL schema validation, MD5 checksum generation, template version enforcement, and regulatory format checks are performed by deterministic libraries — not language models. These gates produce pass/fail outcomes with zero ambiguity, as regulators require.

Human authority at every submission boundary

No return is filed without explicit human approval. Board-level sign-off gates are enforced in the workflow — timestamped, attributed, and retained. The system cannot bypass an approval step, regardless of the confidence level of the AI agent.

Complete auditability from source to submission

Every action — agent or human — is logged with full attribution. When the PRA queries a return or Lloyd's requests evidence of governance, the complete chain of custody is available immediately: what data was pulled, how it was validated, who approved it, and when it was filed.
